# Density Functional Theory Course

Density Functional Theory (DFT) is one of the most powerful theoretical frameworks for studying
the **electronic structure of atoms, molecules, and solids**. It is widely used in
condensed matter physics, quantum chemistry, and materials science to predict structural,
electronic, magnetic, and optical properties of materials.

This course provides a **systematic introduction to Density Functional Theory**, starting from the
many-body Schrödinger equation and building up to the modern Kohn–Sham formalism used in
state-of-the-art electronic structure calculations.

## Course Outline

This course covers the theoretical and computational foundations of Density Functional Theory.

1. Foundations of quantum many-body physics
2. Born–Oppenheimer approximation
3. Hartree and Hartree–Fock methods
4. Hohenberg–Kohn theorems
5. Kohn–Sham equations
6. Exchange–correlation functionals
8. Electronic structure methods in modern materials science
9. Advanced topics: DFT+U, hybrid functionals, and spin-dependent DFT
7. Practical Density Functional Theory calculations

## What you will learn

Participants will learn:

- The **foundations of Density Functional Theory**
- How the **Hohenberg–Kohn theorems** establish the density as a fundamental variable
- The derivation and interpretation of the **Kohn–Sham equations**
- How **exchange–correlation functionals** are constructed
- How to perform **practical electronic-structure calculations**
- How DFT is used in **materials science, chemistry, and condensed matter physics**

## Program overview

Density Functional Theory is an indispensable tool in modern computational physics and materials science.
It allows researchers to study interacting electron systems by mapping the many-body problem
onto an effective single-particle framework.

This course introduces the **conceptual foundations and practical implementation of DFT**.
Participants will learn how theoretical principles translate into real-world
electronic structure calculations used in modern research.

The material progresses from basic quantum mechanics concepts to advanced DFT
techniques used in contemporary computational materials science.
